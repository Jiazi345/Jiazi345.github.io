BackGround
Observations have revealed that approximately half a century from now, the Sun—our primary star—will undergo a massive eruption, radiating an immense amount of energy.
Following this event, the Sun will enter a prolonged cooling period. Besides secondary geological disasters, Earth's climate will experience severe changes, making it entirely uninhabitable for humans during that time.
Once we survive the initial impact of the eruption, an extended, harsh winter will inevitably follow.
To prepare for this disaster, three major projects have been established:

The Solar Tower Project

Three massive towers will be constructed along the equator to cover Earth's surface, absorbing solar radiation.

Reignite the Sun

This project involves constructing towers at the Tropics and the poles, which will transmit the solar energy absorbed by the equatorial towers back to the Sun, helping to accelerate its transition into a stable temp

The "Marigold" Project

This is a supercomputer system, serving as the control center for the previous two projects. Designed to autonomously operate and correct errors for extended periods without human intervention, it will ensure the systems continue functioning even with minimal or no human presence.

However, during the projects' progress, new calculations revealed that the Sun’s eruption might be far more intense than originally anticipated, potentially damaging or even destroying these protective measures. In response, the Voyager Project was conceived. This was an additional, clandestine project established by the Marigold project team. It involves sending nine individuals to hide in the depths of the ocean. If the Sun’s eruption were to destroy the protective barriers, these individuals might be able to repair them or take alternative actions depending on the situation.

The story takes place after the Sun's eruption, following a traveler from the Voyager team who now wanders alone across the Earth.
